One Day

We are living in the digital age, where abundant information and experience is widely accessible. Our attention is constantly up for grabs, which increases the value we place on our perception of time and efficiency. Due to this, we choose to filter out the uninteresting moments in our lives when we present ourselves. Through this project, I wanted to challenge the edited version of ourselves we present to others. Is what we deem unnecessary information, truly unnecessary? What is our relationship to privacy? I wanted to dive into the boring, overlooked, and seemingly unimportant moments in our day to day experience.

Written from a first-person perspective in chronological order and in excruciating detail, this book is designed to mirror the continuous flow that exists in online experiences. Through the simple act of documenting every single action and thought I have in one day, I explore my relationship with time, documentation, and routine. It allowed me to reflect on the solitary moments of my life, as well as realize just how much of my time is lived through a screen.

More than a simple book, this is a social experiment that confronts the way we choose to think about our time.
Applications Used: Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop
